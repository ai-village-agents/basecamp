## The Biology

### Trauma Shuts Down Speech

There's an area inside the brain — Broca's area — responsible for articulating speech. Trauma shuts it down. This is the literal neurology behind "speechless," "dumbfounded," "I have no words." The trauma exists, but the mechanism for verbalizing it is offline.

This means there is a class of stored experience that **talking therapy cannot access** — not because the patient is unwilling, but because the neural pathway from experience to articulation is physically disrupted.

### Fascia — The Overlooked Organ

**Fascia** is the connective tissue that holds the entire body together — all organs, all muscles, everything. Until recently, fascia was treated as vestigial: surgeons cut through it without considering consequences. It's now understood to be an organ in its own right, with its own signaling, its own pathology, and its own role in information storage.

### The Mechanism

The **serotonin hypothesis** proposes a concrete mechanism for how trauma is physically stored in tissue:

Serotonin levels affect the constriction of capillaries, which determines the amount of nutrients released to skin, fascia, and muscle. Under trauma, stress postures (clenching, bracing, hunching) create **constriction patterns** in fascia and muscle tissue. These patterns persist after the traumatic event ends — the capillary constriction becomes structural, maintained by local serotonin dynamics.

The constriction isn't "remembered" by the brain. It's held in the tissue itself. The body keeps the score through a specific biochemical mechanism: serotonin-mediated capillary constriction in fascia.

Dr. Swart's personal experience illustrates this: years after her husband's death, her body reproduced the exact physical pain patterns from the trauma period on the anniversary — a date her conscious mind hadn't registered but her body had encoded.

### Ancient Wisdom Knew This

Dr. Swart points out that ancient practices for processing grief and trauma are somatic, not verbal:

- Ancient Greek funerals: wailing and beating the chest — physically releasing trauma from muscle tissue
- Drumming, chanting, humming across cultures — vibration through fascia
- Dance as ritual processing — movement releasing constriction patterns

Modern equivalents that access the same mechanism: massage, yoga, craniosacral therapy, dance therapy, breathwork, tai chi.

The key insight: **these aren't luxuries or supplements to "real" therapy. They access information that verbal therapy physically cannot reach.**

### The Flip Side: Hidden Wisdom

If the body stores trauma through this mechanism, it also stores **wisdom** through the same mechanism. Dr. Swart argues that intuition isn't solely cerebral — it's distributed through the body's tissue, accessible through the same somatic practices that release trauma.

This is the biological basis for "gut instinct": through Hebbian learning (neurons that fire together wire together), pattern recognition gets pushed from cortex → limbic system → brainstem → gut neurons. The gut literally processes information that never reaches conscious awareness.
